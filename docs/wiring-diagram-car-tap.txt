
  ETHANOL CONTENT ANALYZER - IN-CAR TAP WIRING DIAGRAM
  =====================================================

  Tapping into an existing Flex Fuel sensor already installed in the vehicle.
  The ECU keeps its original connection — the ESP32 eavesdrops on the signal.


  VEHICLE WIRING (tap into the sensor connector)
  ================================================

  FLEX FUEL SENSOR                              ECU

  | Pin 1: 12V Power |--+---> 12V IGN      |           |
  | Pin 2: Ground    |--+---> Chassis GND  |           |
  | Pin 3: Signal    |==+==================|  Signal   |
  +------------------+  ||  |              |  Input    |
                        ||  |              +-----------+
                        ||  |
                  TAP ALL 3 PINS
                   (T-splice x3)

  PARTS LIST (for car tap installation)
  ======================================
  1x  ESP32-C3 Super Mini
  1x  DC-DC Buck Converter module (12V to 5V, e.g. Mini-360 or MP1584)
  1x  R1 - 10kOhm resistor
  1x  R2 - 20kOhm resistor
  3x  T-splice / scotch lock / solder tap (12V, GND, signal)
  --  Wire, heat shrink, connectors as needed


  IMPORTANT NOTES
  ===============
  - Do NOT cut the existing signal wire. Tap alongside it so the ECU
    still receives the sensor signal uninterrupted.
  - The sensor's 12V line is already ignition-switched, so the ESP32
    will power on/off with the car automatically.
  - Keep all grounds common (vehicle chassis, sensor, DC-DC, ESP32).
  - Keep wires between the tap and voltage divider short to reduce
    noise pickup in the engine bay.
  - The voltage divider (10k + 20k = 30kOhm) draws ~0.17 mA from the
    signal line — negligible and won't affect the ECU reading.
  - Optionally add a 100nF ceramic capacitor between GPIO4 and GND
    close to the ESP32 for extra noise filtering.
